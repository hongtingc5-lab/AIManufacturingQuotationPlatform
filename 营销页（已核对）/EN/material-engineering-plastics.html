Engineering Plastics

Non-metal materials for lightweight, insulation, low friction, chemical resistance or appearance prototypes.

about 1.0-1.5Common density g/cm³
material dependentStrength range
GoodCNC / molding
material dependentHeat / chemical resistance

Selection Focus

Engineering plastics are useful for weight reduction, insulation, wear behavior and fast validation. POM is dimensionally stable with low friction, PA is tough but moisture absorption matters, PC has impact resistance, and PEEK improves heat and chemical resistance at a higher cost.

POM, PA, PC, PEEK, ABSBuilt to drawingMaterial documents on requestReviewed with process route

Common Grades and Use Guidance

Grade / TypeCommon useNotes
POMSliders, gears, guide partsLow friction and good dimensional stability
PA / NylonWear parts, cushions, structuresGood toughness; moisture-related size change matters
PEEKHeat, chemical and high-performance partsHigher cost; suitable for demanding conditions

Suitable processes

CNC / Sheet metal / FormingThe route should be selected by grade, geometry and order quantity.

Required inputs

CAD + drawing + material notesGrade, condition, finishing and acceptance requirements should be stated clearly.

Risk control

Review before productionThin walls, deep cavities, welding and cosmetic requirements should be checked early.

Engineering Plastics FAQ

These answers help clarify fit, input files and quotation review before submitting a manufacturing request.

What projects is Engineering Plastics suitable for?

Engineering Plastics is suitable for projects that need manufacturing review based on drawings, material, quantity, tolerances and delivery goals, especially prototypes, low-volume parts and functional components.

What information is needed for a Engineering Plastics quote?

Prepare 3D files, 2D drawings when tolerances matter, material grade, quantity, finish requirements, inspection needs and target delivery timing so engineers can evaluate the route accurately.

How are lead time and cost confirmed for Engineering Plastics?

Lead time and cost are confirmed after reviewing geometry, material, process route, finishing, inspection scope and order quantity. Complex tolerances or special finishes may require engineering confirmation.
