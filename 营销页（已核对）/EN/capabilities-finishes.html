Surface Finishing Processes

Common finishing routes are organized around appearance, corrosion protection, wear behavior, conductivity, traceability and clean stability, with material, masking, thickness, color and inspection needs confirmed before production.

Filter by finishing goal

Anodizing

A common aluminum finish that improves corrosion resistance, wear behavior and appearance options such as clear or black.

Learn more

Electroplating

Zinc, nickel, chrome, tin and other plating routes improve corrosion resistance, conductivity, wear or appearance.

Learn more

Painting

Supports color, gloss, insulating protection and masked areas on metal and selected plastic parts.

Learn more

Powder Coating

Good for metal housings, brackets and sheet-metal parts with thicker coating, weather resistance and stable colors.

Learn more

Passivation

Stabilizes stainless steel surfaces by removing free-iron contamination and improving corrosion performance.

Learn more

Heat Treatment

Quenching, tempering, aging and related routes improve hardness, strength or dimensional stability.

Learn more

Laser Marking

Permanent serial numbers, QR codes, logos and traceability marks for prototype and batch parts.

Learn more

Polishing

Improves surface smoothness, touch and local reflectivity for cosmetic parts, sealing faces and sliding contact areas.

Learn more

Black Oxide

Adds a black appearance and basic rust protection for steel fixtures, bushings, transmission and mechanical parts.

Learn more

Common Finishing Selection Reference

ProcessMaterial fitMain goalDesign note
AnodizingAluminum alloysAppearance, corrosion and wear resistanceThickness and color depend on alloy and process
ElectroplatingSteel, copper, aluminum and other metalsCorrosion, conductivity, wear or decorative goalsDeep holes, sharp edges and fit faces need review
Painting / Powder coatingMetals and selected plasticsColor, insulation and weather resistanceHigher film build; threads and fit faces need masking
PassivationStainless steelSurface stability and corrosion improvementNot a coating; appearance change is normally slight
Heat treatmentSteel, aluminum and selected alloysHardness, strength and dimensional stabilityDistortion can occur; machining sequence matters
