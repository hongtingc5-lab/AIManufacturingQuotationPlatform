DFM Manufacturability Check

Identify wall thickness, hole depth, chamfer, clamping, material and post-processing risks before quoting and production, making it easier for designs to enter manufacturing.

Wall thickness analysisHole Depth AssessmentTolerance recommendationsFeedback before quotation

What We Check

DFM results are used to assist manufacturing decisions, and the final processing plan still needs to be confirmed in conjunction with drawings, materials, equipment and engineering.

Structural Risks

  • Minimum wall thickness, deep cavities, sharp corners and weak areas.
  • Hole depth, tool accessibility and clamping interference.

Cost Risks

  • Complex features, tight tolerances and special post-processing.
  • Lot quantities, expedited requirements and inspection add-ons.

Delivery Risks

  • Material availability, Surface Finishing cycles and re-inspection requirements.
  • Files are missing, and drawings and model information are inconsistent.

Review Workflow

01
Upload models and drawingsSupports establishing basic information by model, drawing, material, quantity and purpose.
02
AI preliminary recognitionIdentify geometric features and significant risks to form preliminary manufacturability hints.
03
Project reviewReview key items in conjunction with equipment, cutting tools, processes and testing requirements.
04
Feedback and suggestionsOutput modification suggestions, alternative processes and factors that may affect cost/delivery.
